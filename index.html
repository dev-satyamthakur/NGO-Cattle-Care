About Us

Mission and Vision

At the Sri Radha Krishna Mandir Gaushala Trust, our mission is to provide a safe and loving haven for abandoned and distressed cows, while fostering a harmonious coexistence between these gentle beings and our communities. We are committed to ensuring the welfare, protection, and preservation of cows, which are an integral part of our cultural heritage and a source of numerous blessings.

Our Story

In the early years, a group of like-minded individuals, bound by their love for cows and a strong spiritual connection to the divine, came together to address the pressing issue of abandoned and distressed cows on Indian roads. It was a call to action that emerged from the very heart of our culture and spirituality, rooted in the teachings of Lord Krishna, who held the cow in the highest regard.

The Present and Beyond

Today, the Sri Radha Krishna Mandir Gaushala Trust stands as a symbol of hope and compassion. We continue to provide shelter, care, and love to abandoned cows, while spreading awareness about the profound significance of cows in our culture and environment. Our story is not just a history but a testament to our enduring commitment to nurturing this divine connection.

Issues

Littering on Roads

Littering on roads poses a significant threat to the well-being of cows and the environment. Discarded waste, particularly plastic, can be ingested by cows, leading to severe health issues.

Abandoned Cows

Cows, symbols of divinity in India, often endure a stark contrast in reality. Abandoned on the streets, these once-cherished beings face dangers, malnutrition, cruelty, and suffering.

Cow-Related Distress

Cows wandering on roads pose challenges for both these gentle beings and people. Abandoned and distressed cows face accidents, malnutrition, and illness.

Solutions

Sarvajanik Bhojnalaya
for Cows

A "Sarvajanik Bhojnalaya" for cows, also known as a community feeding program for cows, is a concept rooted in Indian culture and tradition. It involves the practice of providing food to cows, often in a communal setting, for both religious and humanitarian reasons. This tradition is deeply intertwined with the reverence for cows in Hinduism, where they are considered sacred and revered as "gau mata" (mother cow).

What Cows Give

Milk

Cow's milk is a nutritional powerhouse, rich in calcium, protein, and essential vitamins. It is a primary source of nourishment for people of all ages.

Ghee

The process of making ghee, or clarified butter, from cow's milk is an age-old tradition. Ghee is renowned for its culinary and medicinal properties, enhancing the flavors of dishes and supporting holistic well-being.

Dung

Cow dung is a versatile resource. It's used as an organic fertilizer, promoting healthy crop growth, and as a clean-burning fuel for cooking. Additionally, it's utilized to craft eco-friendly products like cow dung cakes.

Manure

Cow dung is a valuable source of organic fertilizer, promoting healthy crop growth and sustainable agriculture.

Agricultural Support

Cows play an essential role in traditional agriculture. They help plow fields and assist in crop cultivation, making them valuable partners in food production.
